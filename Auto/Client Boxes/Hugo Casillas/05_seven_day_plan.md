Call Prep (when it happens)
The call should run ~30 min, with the fiancée on. Andre's job:

First 5 min — discovery, not pitch. "Before I walk through anything, what's been the trickiest thing about planning this wedding so far?" Listen. Mirror. Don't sell.
Min 5–15 — vegetarian/gluten execution. Lead with the fiancée — name her, look at her (or her side of the screen), ask her what dishes have made her feel like an afterthought at other weddings she's been to. Then walk through the cauliflower steak, the dedicated prep station, the gluten-free protocols. Own this part. She's the deciding voice on dietary.
Min 15–25 — Highland Orchard logistics. Reference the venue's setup, confirm the load-in time, mention that we've worked the venue before (verify). This is the trust moment.
Min 25–30 — close. "What would have to be true for you to feel ready to lock the date?" If the answer is anything other than "we need a few more days," respond directly. If it's "we need a few more days," ask: "what specifically — price, dietary, or something else?" Pin it down.
Don't quote. Don't negotiate. He has the quote. The negotiation conversation comes after he's said yes in spirit. If he opens pricing, deflect: "Let's settle the menu and the day first. We can talk numbers once we're aligned on what we're actually doing."
