### Day 1 — Today (Saturday) — SMS to Elizabeth — late afternoon (~5pm ET)

**Why this move:**

- *"I missed you ... and that's on me"* — owns the failure cleanly. **Military-trained people respect accountability without grovel.** She'll read this as professional, not weak. Vendors who pretend nothing happened don't get a second chance from people in her cohort.
- *"In a window that works for you"* — flips control back to her. She set a window yesterday and we missed it. The remedy isn't *"let me try calling again"* — it's *"you tell me when, I'll be there."*
- *"15-min slot"* — sets a tight, respectful container. Discovery calls don't need to be 30-60 minutes; 15 minutes shows we know what we're doing. **Military planners hate meetings that overrun.**
- *"In the next few business days"* — implies weekday business hours, which matches her professional register. Doesn't push for tonight or tomorrow weekend.
- *"The gluten-free menu and the Officers' Club logistics specifically"* — **this is the line that earns the reply.** It tells her *we listened to your inquiry. We know what you actually care about. We're not going to waste your time on a generic discovery call.* Specificity = respect.
- *"No rush"* — releases pressure. **For a 16-month-out wedding, urgency manufactured by us reads as desperation. Patience reads as confidence.**
- 60 words. Tight. No emojis. Capitalized properly. Done.

**Off-ramps:**

- 🟢 **She replies with a time slot:** Confirm immediately and concisely. *"Got it — Tuesday at 12:30 works. I'll call your number on file. Talk then."* Then **prep for the call** (see Call Prep below) and *don't text again until the call happens.*

## Call Prep (when she schedules one)

This call is the moment of truth. Andre needs to walk in *prepared* the way a military officer would expect a vendor to be prepared.

**Before the call:**

1. **Gluten-free protocol confirmed in writing.** Andre needs to know — *not guess* — what the kitchen protocol is for GF. Dedicated prep area? Separate utensils? Allergen training certifications? **Whatever the actual answer is, that's what Andre says on the call. No upselling our capabilities.**
2. **Base vendor-approval status checked.** Has Comeketo catered Naval Station Newport before? If yes, what was the approval process and is the approval still active? If no, what's the path? **Andre needs the real answer before the call.**
3. **Officers' Club logistics homework done.** Pull the venue's standard event guide, load-in restrictions, security requirements. Show up knowing the venue.
4. **Print the menu options.** Offer her two clean configurations: standard cocktail-hour + buffet, and an upgraded version with a carving station or live element. **Per-person pricing, all-in numbers including travel.**

**During the call (15-20 min, no longer):**

1. **Open with her — not us.** *"Elizabeth, before I tell you anything about us — what's the picture you and Peter have for the day? And I want to make sure we cover the gluten-free side first, since you mentioned it."*
2. **Ask one question about who's military.** *"I noticed the Officers' Club venue — congratulations. Are you or Peter the military connection, or is it through family?"* This is the question that cracks open the relationship dynamic. **Don't assume which one of them is the service member.** Many female officers exist; many male civilians do too.
3. **Listen for the GF specifics.** Celiac vs. sensitivity? Cross-contamination level? Have past caterers fumbled it? **Whatever she tells you, repeat it back.** *"Got it — celiac, full cross-contamination protocol needed. We'll build the menu around that."* That repeat-back move is huge — it tells her she's being heard.
4. **Walk through the cocktail-hour-heavy + buffet vision.** Her Zola note already laid this out. Confirm we got it right. *"You mentioned heavy cocktail-hour appetizers and a buffet — I want to make sure I'm hearing that right. Are we picturing 10-12 passed apps, or fewer with stations, or some mix?"*
5. **Address the base logistics with confidence + specifics.** *"Naval Station Newport has its own vendor approval process. Here's where we stand on that..."* — say the actual truth, whatever it is.
6. **Don't quote precisely. Anchor.** *"For 80 guests with the cocktail-heavy + buffet vision, you're looking at $13-15K depending on how the apps and bar shake out. I'll send a clean two-option side-by-side after this call so you and Peter can compare."*
7. **Close with the next step.** *"Two natural next steps. Want to come to a tasting May 17 in Fitchburg — about 80 minutes from Newport — or would you rather have me send the side-by-side first and decide from there?"* **Two paths, her call.**

**After the call:**

Send the promised side-by-side document within 24 hours. **Hand-built, not templated.** It should reflect *what she said on the call*, not generic Comeketo packages. Include the GF protocol in writing. Include base-logistics notes. Include real per-person and total numbers.
