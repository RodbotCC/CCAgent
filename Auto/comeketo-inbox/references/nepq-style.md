# NEPQ-Style Messaging — Catering Inbox

NEPQ (Neuro-Emotional Persuasion Questions) is the messaging standard for Comeketo's inbox work. The shape of every outbound message comes from this style.

## Core Principle

**Ask, don't pitch.** A NEPQ message is a question wrapped in calm, grounded curiosity. It pulls the lead forward instead of pushing them. The lead does the talking; the rep does the listening.

## What NEPQ Does

- Surfaces the lead's actual situation
- Reveals what they're really trying to figure out
- Makes it easier for them to say "yes," "no," or "I'm not sure" — all three are wins, because all three move the deal forward
- Avoids the salesy energy that makes leads ghost

## What NEPQ Avoids

- Heavy benefits-talk ("we're the best at X")
- Generic re-engagement ("just checking in!")
- False urgency ("limited spots left, act now")
- Questions the lead has already answered
- Restating what was already pitched

## Three Question Patterns That Work

### 1. Situation question
> "Just so I understand where things stand, are you mostly trying to get clarity on **budget**, **menu direction**, or **service style** right now?"

Used when the lead has gone quiet and the rep doesn't know what's blocking them. The three-option frame makes it easy to answer.

### 2. Clarity question
> "What are you still trying to get clear on before it makes sense to jump on a quick call and line this up the right way?"

Used when there's been back-and-forth but no commitment. Surfaces the actual hesitation.

### 3. Choice question
> "Would it make more sense to lock a quick call first, or should I save you a spot for one of our tastings on May 3 at 5:30 PM or May 17 at 2:00 PM?"

Used when both paths are viable and the rep wants the lead to pick the lane. The phrasing ("would it make more sense") lowers commitment friction.

## Tone Calibration

- **Calm, not eager.** The rep has options, they're not chasing.
- **Curious, not pushy.** Genuine interest in what the lead actually wants.
- **Specific, not vague.** Reference actual past touches, real dates, real context.
- **Short.** SMS: one to three sentences. Email body: a few short paragraphs max.
- **One question per message.** Two questions split the lead's attention and lower reply rates.

## Message Anatomy

Every NEPQ message has roughly three beats:

1. **Anchor** — a brief reference to the last touch or the current situation
2. **Question** — the NEPQ pull
3. **Easy out** — a low-friction way to respond

Example breakdown:

> **Anchor:** Hey Tatiana, I looked back at our last couple of touches and saw a text about "Please text me" and an email about service types.
>
> **Question:** What are you still trying to get clear on before it makes sense to jump on a quick call?
>
> **Easy out:** If it's easier, send me a time that works and I'll call you.

## When to Skip the Question and Just Pull Forward

If the lead has been on the fence for multiple touches, the question pattern can wear thin. In that case, replace the question with a **gentle either/or**:

> "Hey Tatiana — if a quick call is easier, I can make time at 10:00 AM, 11:00 AM, or 1:00 PM. Or if a tasting feels closer to right, May 3 at 5:30 PM is open."

This still respects NEPQ (it's a choice, not a pitch) but stops asking the lead to define their problem if they've shown they don't want to.

## Anti-Patterns to Catch in Drafts

- ❌ "Just following up to see if you had any questions!" — generic filler.
- ❌ "Our churrasco is the best in Boston!" — pitch, not pull.
- ❌ "I noticed you haven't replied..." — guilt energy, kills the thread.
- ❌ Restating tasting dates verbatim from a prior message — feels like a copy-paste, lowers reply rate.
- ❌ Multiple questions stacked — pick one.
- ❌ Emoji-heavy — Comeketo tone is grounded, not chirpy. One occasional emoji is fine; three or more is too much.

## When the Inbox Task Is a Ballpark Quote

The ballpark email template carries its own NEPQ moment built into the body:

> "Just so I understand where things stand — are you mostly trying to get clarity on budget, menu direction, or service style right now?"
>
> "Which option feels closer to what you want?"

Don't add a second NEPQ question on top. Let the template do its job. The rep's only customization is making sure the **pitch line** under each option name is specific to *this* lead's event — not boilerplate.
