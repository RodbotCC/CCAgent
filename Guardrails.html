A · The non-negotiables

Hard Gates

If any of these is violated, the system does not send. It surfaces the issue in your DM and waits for you (Andre) or Jake to clear it.

Hard GateA1
Ownership — only Andre's leads
Source of truth: the LEAD OWNER field in Close = 01. 😎 Andre.
If anyone else owns the lead, no email, no SMS, no quote, no tasting invite. Even if the lead came in through Andre's phone number.
Hard GateA2
Status — Won and Lost are no-touch
No outbound on Won or Lost leads, ever, for any reason. Inbox tasks can still be moved on a Won/Lost lead, but no message goes out.

"Probably Not" is NOT a no-touch status. If a "Probably Not" lead has had inbound activity in the last 14 days, surface it for re-evaluation rather than treating it as deprioritized. (This is the lesson from the Daphney/Frankie + Elizabeth/Peter misclassifications.)
Hard GateA3
Calendar reality — explicit dates only
Before any send, every relative date in the message ("Sunday," "tomorrow," "this afternoon," "next week") gets re-anchored against today's actual calendar.

Bare day-of-week labels get replaced with explicit dates: Sunday May 3, not Sunday. Never claim an action that didn't happen on the date the message implies.
Origin: the Flávia "Sunday at 11 AM" recovery on April 27.
Hard GateA6
Hard-blocked tasting date
Sunday, April 19 is permanently off the map. Don't send that date again, ever, under any circumstance.
B · Things that pause and ask

Commitment & Enrichment

Auto-PauseB1
Contractual commitments — never silently fire
No outbound message can contain fee waivers, free services, price guarantees, scope expansions, or any commitment that touches money / headcount / terms — unless Andre or Jake explicitly approved it at plan-write time.

Trigger phrases that freeze a send

freewaiveno chargecomplimentaryon uswon't chargecover thethrowing inup to N peopleguaranteedlock indiscount% off
On a hit, the message freezes in your DM as [COMMITMENT FLAG] and waits for your YES, a rewrite, or a kill.
Origin: the Brenda & Steve fee-waiver fire on April 27.
Auto-PauseB2
Enrichment use boundary — steer, don't surface
Specific facts about a lead — their industry, employer, role, business, family details, school, history — cannot appear in customer-facing copy unless that fact has been shared with us in actual comms.

Enrichment guides tone and steers the conversation. It cannot be quoted at the lead.

Allowed (steering) "Since you mentioned the tasting on Saturday…"
Not allowed (surfacing) "I know you've been in food service a long time…"

If a draft surfaces a fact not in comms, the system freezes as [ENRICHMENT BOUNDARY] and waits.

What counts as "they shared this with us"

  • Inbound messages from the lead (SMS, email, chat)
  • Phone-call notes from a real conversation
  • Intake-form data the lead self-entered (their name, event date, guest count, venue, dietary notes, budget if typed)
  • Prior outbound the lead actively engaged with
Origin: the Brenda & Steve enrichment-quote fire on April 27.
Auto-PauseB3
Co-decider handling
When a lead has multiple named contacts (Brenda & Steve, Daphney & Frankie, Elizabeth & Peter, etc.), address whoever last replied. Reference the other partner only by the role they've established in the thread ("your fiancée," "Steve" if he's been mentioned in their replies). Don't introduce the partner into the message if the lead hasn't.
C · Frequency, replies, and missed commitments

Cadence & Reply

Hard GateC1
Reply gate — inbound pauses cadence
Any inbound from the lead (SMS, email, or call answered) immediately pauses all queued outbound on that lead. The system surfaces a "lead replied — review next move" alert in your DM. The next scheduled message does not fire until you clear it.
Why: prevents Day-4 SMS firing after a Day-3 reply that changed the picture.
Hard GateC2
Frequency cap per lead
  • Maximum 1 outbound per lead per 24 hours — unless the plan explicitly schedules a same-day combined SMS+email packet (counts as 1 move).
  • Maximum 4 outbound per lead per rolling 7-day window — without Andre's explicit re-authorization.
Hard cap. No system-level overrides.
Origin: the Eliana Lopes 9-contacts-in-4-hours over-eagerness on Day 1.
Auto-PauseC3
Stop signal — opt-out language ends the cadence
If the lead's message contains opt-out language, all queued outbound is canceled, lead status updates to Lost (reason: "opt-out"), and a log entry is created.

Opt-out triggers

stopunsubscriberemove medon't contactgoing with someone elseno longer interestedplease stoptake me offdo not text
The system never sends a "sorry to hear that" or "please reconsider" follow-up. Silence is the response.
Hard GateC4
Commitment tracker — your time-locked promises
When a plan contains a time-locked commitment Andre made to a lead ("I'll call you at 11 AM Sunday," "I'll send the proposal by Tuesday at noon"), the system tracks that commitment as a separate object.
  • T-minus 30 minutes: reminder ping in your DM
  • T-plus 5 minutes after: if no completion logged, system surfaces MISSED COMMITMENT as a critical alert
Origin: the Flávia missed-call recovery. The system didn't catch it; you did. Now it catches it.
D · How and when we send

Channel & Send Window

StandardD1
Language — English by default
Customer-facing language is English unless you explicitly say switch to Portuguese or Spanish for a specific lead.

Never assume a language switch just because the lead has a Brazilian, Portuguese, or Spanish name. The Esther Manu Portuguese-text fire on April 24 was the structural origin of this rule.
StandardD2
SMS vs Email routing
  • If you say "send a text message," SMS only. If there's no valid SMS route on that lead, surface it and ask. Don't silently fall back to email.
  • Otherwise: SMS when there's a number, email when there isn't.
  • Combined SMS + email packets when the plan calls for it (one acknowledgment, both fire after).
Hard GateD3
Outbound send window
  • SMS: 9:00 AM – 7:00 PM lead's local time
  • Email: 7:00 AM – 9:00 PM lead's local time
  • Sunday SMS: allowed only after 11:00 AM lead-local
Outside the window: queue, don't send.

F · No plain-text emails, ever

Email Standard

Hard GateF1
HTML rich-template is mandatory
Plain text and plain-paragraph emails are forbidden. Every email goes through one of two bundled renderers:
  • Ballpark quotes (precise two-option pricing) → quote-table template
  • Everything else (resets, follow-ups, tasting confirmations, mid-cadence nudges, post-call wrap-ups) → follow-up template

Pre-send check: the email body must validate against template structure. If the body is plain text or partial HTML, the send is blocked and surfaces in your DM as [HTML_FAIL] with the un-rendered body for review.

Origin: Andre's WhatsApp screenshot on April 27 showing a plain-text Gabriela quote that should have been HTML.
StandardF2
Auto-included blocks
Every email auto-includes:
  • The calculator-link band
  • The current-cycle tasting card with all three Sundays
Unless explicitly suppressed (e.g. when one specific tasting is already locked in).
StandardF3
One email, not two
If the move includes both a quote and a tasting invite, that's one email. No splitting into a quote email then a separate tasting-only email unless you explicitly say so.
StandardF4
Ballpark guest count fallback
If guest count is missing and a working base is on file, use it. Current fallback: 50 guests.
G · The Andre filter

Voice & Style

StandardG1
NEPQ-style — ask, don't pitch
Calm, grounded curiosity. Pull for clarity instead of pushing hard. Reads like Andre at ceiling, or it doesn't go.
StandardG2
Two-step probe
Every outbound message tries to move the lead toward, in this order:
  1. A phone call with you
  2. The next tasting
Even soft-out / silence-only / acknowledgment messages leave one of those doors open.
StandardG3
Default email structure
Phone-call ask in the body. Tasting card with the three current Sundays at the bottom. Plans can override this if there's a reason.
Auto-PauseG4
Voice profile pre-send check
Every system-composed message is checked against Andre's voice profile before send.
Pass
Capitalized opener. Max 1 exclamation per message. Specific over generic. Reads like Andre at ceiling.
Fail (rewrite or hold)
AI-tells: "I hope this email finds you well." "Please don't hesitate." "I wanted to reach out to let you know." Multiple exclamations. Generic filler.
On fail: rewrite or hold. No template smell.
StandardG5
We want a response
Even if the response is "yes call," "yes tasting," "no thanks," or "stop contacting me." A non-response message is a missed move.
H · The flow per inbox task

Decision Tree

This is the order the system runs, every time.

  1. Owner = Andre? If no, stop.
  2. Status = Won or Lost? If yes, stop.
  3. Lead replied since last outbound? If yes, pause cadence, surface for review.
  4. Read recent thread context.
  5. Channel choice: SMS-only if you said text-only; otherwise SMS-or-email by route.
  6. Send-window check: if outside hours, queue, don't send.
  7. Compose: one NEPQ-style message in English, two-step probe toward call + tasting.
  8. Tasting block: use only the current cycle's dates if tasting is in the move.
  9. Calendar reality check: replace bare day-of-week with explicit date.
  10. Frequency cap check: 1/24h, 4/7d. If over, hold and surface.
  11. Commitment scan: trigger-phrase hit → freeze as [COMMITMENT FLAG], wait for your call.
  12. Enrichment boundary check: every lead-fact must trace to comms. Hit → freeze as [ENRICHMENT BOUNDARY].
  13. HTML check (email only): body must validate against template. Fail → freeze as [HTML_FAIL].
  14. Voice check: capitalized opener, no AI-tells, ≤1 exclamation. Fail → rewrite or hold.
  15. Send.
  16. Move the task per your instruction.
  17. Log it: what was sent, what was skipped, what failed, which tasks moved. If the run isn't reported, the run is incomplete.
One-page reference for the inbox

If anything in here happens, the system holds and pings you:

[COMMITMENT FLAG]   fee waiver / free / waive
[ENRICHMENT BOUNDARY]   fact not in comms
[HTML_FAIL]   plain-text email blocked
MISSED COMMITMENT   you didn't honor a time-lock

What the system never does on its own:

  • Touch a non-Andre lead
  • Message Won or Lost
  • Send Portuguese or Spanish without your say-so
  • Send plain-text email
  • Surface enrichment as if the lead told us
  • Send after a stop signal
  • Fire more than 1 message per lead per 24 hours
  • Send outside the 9–7 SMS / 7–9 email window

What you (Andre) own:

  • Approving fee waivers, free services, scope expansions
  • Honoring time-locked call commitments
  • Telling the system the current tasting dates each cycle
  • Switching language from English on a specific lead
  • Clearing held messages from your DM queue

When in doubt — surface to Andre, don't fire.
